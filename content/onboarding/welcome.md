# Welcome to the team

You've joined the model analytics job family — the group that builds, documents, monitors, and defends the quantitative models used across the commercial business. This page is your starting point; everything it references lives in Atlas, so you can always find your way back.

## What this team does

- **Build and maintain models** — from development through validation and ongoing monitoring. Each model has its own space in the [Models channel](/models/index.md) with its whitepaper, user guide, and monitoring plan side by side.
- **Keep the paper trail defensible** — every number we publish traces back to a governed document. See [[model-risk-101]] for why this matters more here than almost anywhere else.
- **Learn in public (internally)** — mistakes become [[lessons/index|lessons learned]], not folklore.

## Day-1 checklist

1. Read this page and [[first-90-days]].
2. Skim the [[glossary]] — you'll hit these acronyms in your first standup.
3. Read [[npi-data-handling]] **before** touching any customer extract. Non-negotiable.
4. Read [[genai-acceptable-use]] before pasting anything into an AI tool.
5. Open one model space end-to-end (try the [CRE PD model](/model/cre-pd-v2)) to see how whitepaper → user guide → monitoring plan fit together.
6. Bookmark [[who-to-ask]].

## Your job family

This guide is written for the **Valuation Analyst (VA)** family but the structure is role-agnostic — model developers, validators, and monitoring analysts follow the same ramp with different depth per channel. Your manager will tell you which model spaces to go deep on first.
