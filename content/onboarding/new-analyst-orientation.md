# New Analyst Orientation (deck)

> Generated by the `deck-ingest` skill from the orientation pptx (7 slides + speaker notes) — a demo of turning a deck into a hub page. Slide references point back to the source.

## Summary

The day-one orientation deck for the Model Analytics job family. Its framing: the deck is the guided tour, Atlas is the reference — everything presented lives here (slide 1). It walks new analysts through where knowledge lives, a first-week checklist, the three-document anatomy of every model, monitoring basics, and the escalation culture around wrong-looking numbers.

## Where knowledge lives

Atlas is the front door, organized in the five channels — Onboarding, Models, Compliance, Lessons, Tooling — with one space per model holding whitepaper + user guide + monitoring plan (slide 2). The speaker note sets the norm bluntly: *if a document is not in Atlas or linked from it, treat it as not existing — no shared-drive archaeology.*

## First-week checklist (slide 3)

1. Read [[welcome]] and [[first-90-days]].
2. Skim the [[glossary]].
3. Read the NPI policy **before** touching any extract — per the notes, NPI training precedes any data access request, non-negotiable. See [[npi-data-handling]].
4. Open one model space end to end (e.g. the [CRE PD model](/model/cre-pd-v2)).

## The three documents of every model (slide 4)

- **Whitepaper** — why it works: methodology, assumptions, limitations.
- **User guide** — how to run it: inputs, steps, checks.
- **Monitoring plan** — how we know it still works: metrics, thresholds.

Speaker's analogy: *theory, procedure, and smoke detector.*

## Monitoring in one slide (slide 5)

Green / Amber / Red per metric; "Amber: document within 10 business days"; "Red: same-day notification, remediation plan in 30 days"; "Two consecutive ambers = red". Per the notes these are the CRE PD defaults — each model's own [monitoring plan](/model/cre-pd-v2?tab=Monitoring%20Plan) is authoritative — and the two-ambers rule came out of [[2024-assumption-drift]].

## When things look wrong (slide 6)

Never fix silently; raise to the model owner and log it; *the wrongness is evidence — do not erase it.* The talk track tells the [[2025-vendor-data-gap]] story: an analyst hand-corrected two scores and erased the strongest signal of a systemic data issue.

## Who to ask (slide 7)

Methodology → model owner; data and NPI → data steward; everything else → team channel, tag the lead. Full routing table: [[who-to-ask]]. Closing line: *misrouted questions are cheap, unasked ones are not.*
