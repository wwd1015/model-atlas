# First 90 days

The ramp is deliberately artifact-driven: at each stage you *produce* something small, because producing is how you find the gaps in your understanding.

## Days 1–30 — Orient

**Goal:** navigate the landscape without a guide.

- Complete the day-1 checklist in [[welcome]].
- Read the whitepaper for your assigned model space end-to-end. Log every term you had to look up — propose missing ones for the [[glossary]].
- Sit in on one monitoring cycle. Follow along in the model's monitoring plan.
- Read the two most recent [[lessons/index|lessons learned]] and be ready to discuss what control would have prevented each.

**Milestone:** walk your manager through your model's whitepaper structure from memory — purpose, methodology, key assumptions, limitations.

## Days 31–60 — Contribute

**Goal:** do supervised production work.

- Run one monitoring cycle yourself, shadowed. The user guide in your model space is the procedure; if it's wrong or stale, *that's a finding* — file it.
- Pick up one small backlog item in the [[atlas-pipeline]] or another tool in the Tooling channel.
- Complete NPI training and confirm you can explain the three rules in [[npi-data-handling]] without looking.

**Milestone:** your monitoring output is accepted with only minor review comments.

## Days 61–90 — Own

**Goal:** own a recurring deliverable.

- Take a monitoring cycle unshadowed.
- Present one lesson learned or tooling improvement at the team forum.
- Draft one new Atlas concept (a how-to, an FAQ entry, a glossary batch) — knowledge you wish had existed on your day 1.

**Milestone:** you are the named owner of at least one recurring artifact.

## If you get stuck

[[who-to-ask]] routes every topic to a person. When in doubt, ask in the team channel — the only bad question in a regulated environment is the one you didn't ask before shipping.
